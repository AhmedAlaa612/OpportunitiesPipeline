The applications are now open for the **Harvard Public Policy Leadership Conference 2026**. It is a **Fully Funded** annual Public Policy Leadership Conference at the Harvard Kennedy School in Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA. The conference lasts **4 days** and will take place from **October 29 to November 1, 2026**. This fully funded leadership conference is designed for undergraduate students interested in public policy, public service, leadership, and government careers. The objective is to inspire students to pursue careers in public policy and become leaders in public service in the future. Through this program, participants will connect with other Leaders to learn from them about Public Policy and Leadership skills. More details are below.

## **Quick Overview**

* **Host Country:** United States
* **Institution:** Harvard Kennedy School
* **Duration:** 4 Days
* **Program:** Public Policy Leadership Conference (PPLC)
* **Conference Dates:** 29 October – 1 November 2026
* **Funding:** Fully Funded
* **Deadline: 10 July 2026**

### **About the Harvard Public Policy Leadership Conference**

The Public Policy Leadership Conference (PPLC) is a flagship program by Harvard Kennedy School aimed at inspiring talented undergraduate students to pursue careers in public policy and public service.

Participants will:

* attend policy discussions and workshops
* engage with Harvard faculty and students
* explore graduate school pathways
* learn about fellowships and scholarships
* network with students from across the United States

### **Financial Benefits**

Harvard Kennedy School covers:

* Round-trip transportation
* Accommodation
* Meals during the conference
* Conference participation costs
* Access to workshops and networking sessions

### **Who Should Apply?**

This opportunity is ideal for:

* Undergraduate students
* Future public policy leaders
* Students interested in government and politics
* Applicants passionate about public service
* Students from underserved or underrepresented backgrounds

### **Eligibility Criteria**

* Be enrolled in a U.S. undergraduate institution
* Have completed at least two academic semesters
* Not graduated during the 2026–2027 academic year
* Have a minimum GPA of 3.0
* Demonstrate commitment to public service and leadership
* Be eligible for a Federal Pell Grant
* Eligible applicants include U.S. citizens, permanent residents, and non-citizen U.S. nationals.

### **Required Documents**

* Online application form
* Résumé
* Personal statement essay
* Letter of recommendation
* Undergraduate academic transcript
